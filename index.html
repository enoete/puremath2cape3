📐 Formula Sheet — Pure Math U2 · Module 3

Conditional Probability

$P(A\mid B)=\dfrac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}$
$P(A\cap B)=P(A)\cdot P(B\mid A)=P(B)\cdot P(A\mid B)$

Independence

Independent $\Leftrightarrow P(A\cap B)=P(A)\cdot P(B)$
Equivalently: $P(A\mid B)=P(A)$

Addition & Complement

$P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)$
$P(A')=1-P(A)$
$P(A'\cup B')=P((A\cap B)')=1-P(A\cap B)$

Total Probability & Bayes

$P(A)=P(A\mid B)P(B)+P(A\mid B')P(B')$
$P(B\mid A)=\dfrac{P(A\mid B)P(B)}{P(A\mid B)P(B)+P(A\mid B')P(B')}$

Determinant & Inverse

$\det\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix}=ad-bc$
$A^{-1}=\dfrac{1}{\det A}\,\text{adj}(A)$; $\;\text{adj}(A)=C^T$ (transpose of cofactor matrix)
Cofactor: $C_{ij}=(-1)^{i+j}M_{ij}$

Singular & Systems

Singular $\Leftrightarrow \det(A)=0$
Unique solution $\Leftrightarrow \det(A)\neq0$
Inconsistent: row $[0\;0\;0\mid k]$, $k\neq0$

Row Operations

$R_i\leftrightarrow R_j$, $\quad kR_i$, $\quad R_i+kR_j$

Integrating Factor Method

Standard form: $\dfrac{dy}{dx}+P(x)y=Q(x)$
$\mu=e^{\int P(x)\,dx}$, then $\dfrac{d}{dx}(\mu y)=\mu Q$
Solution: $\mu y=\int\mu Q\,dx+C$

Separable Equations

$\dfrac{dy}{dx}=f(x)g(y)\implies\int\dfrac{dy}{g(y)}=\int f(x)\,dx$

Useful Integrals

$\int e^{ax}\sin bx\,dx=\dfrac{e^{ax}(a\sin bx-b\cos bx)}{a^2+b^2}+C$
$\int e^{ax}\cos bx\,dx=\dfrac{e^{ax}(a\cos bx+b\sin bx)}{a^2+b^2}+C$

Auxiliary Equation for $ay''+by'+cy=f(x)$

Solve $am^2+bm+c=0$
Two real roots $m_1\neq m_2$: $y_c=Ae^{m_1x}+Be^{m_2x}$
Repeated root $m$: $y_c=(A+Bx)e^{mx}$
Complex $\alpha\pm\beta i$: $y_c=e^{\alpha x}(A\cos\beta x+B\sin\beta x)$

Particular Integral Trials

$ke^{ax}$: try $\lambda e^{ax}$ (modify to $\lambda xe^{ax}$ if $a$ is root)
Poly degree $n$: try $a_nx^n+\cdots+a_0$
$p\sin bx+q\cos bx$: try $\lambda\sin bx+\mu\cos bx$

General Solution

$y=y_c+y_p$

Permutations & Combinations

$^nP_r=\dfrac{n!}{(n-r)!}$
$\dbinom{n}{r}=\dfrac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$

Arrangements with Repeats

$n$ objects, repeats $n_1,n_2,\ldots$: $\dfrac{n!}{n_1!n_2!\cdots}$

Key Strategies

At least 1 = Total $-$ None
Items together: treat as single block, then multiply by internal arrangements
Items separated: Total $-$ (items together)

P1 CAPE 2025 Paper 02 · Q5(a)
Two fair dice are tossed consecutively and the sum recorded. Let E = first die is even; F = sum is less than 9.
  1. Complete the sample space table of all possible sums.
  2. Calculate $P(F\mid E)$.
  3. Determine whether F and E are independent.
💡 Hints
Hint 1 — Sample space
6×6 table, rows = Die 2, columns = Die 1. Each cell = Die1 + Die2. Total = 36 equally likely outcomes.
Hint 2 — Event E
E: Die 1 $\in\{2,4,6\}$ → 18 outcomes. $P(E)=\tfrac{1}{2}$.
Hint 3 — Conditional probability
$P(F\mid E)=\dfrac{P(F\cap E)}{P(E)}$. Count cells where Die 1 is even AND sum $<9$.
Hint 4 — Independence test
Independent iff $P(F\mid E)=P(F)$. Count ALL sums $<9$ for $P(F)$ and compare.
✔ Answer
(ii) Die1=2: all 6 sums (3–8) $<9$. Die1=4: Die2=1,2,3,4 → 4 outcomes. Die1=6: Die2=1,2 → 2 outcomes. $|F\cap E|=12$.
$P(F\mid E)=\dfrac{12/36}{18/36}=\dfrac{12}{18}=\dfrac{2}{3}$
(iii) All sums $<9$: 26 outcomes, so $P(F)=\tfrac{26}{36}=\tfrac{13}{18}$.
Since $\tfrac{2}{3}=\tfrac{12}{18}\neq\tfrac{13}{18}$, F and E are NOT independent.
P2
A chemical test kit is 95% accurate in detecting acidic solutions but gives a 1% false-positive rate. If 0.5% of solutions tested are truly acidic, find $P(\text{acidic} \mid \text{positive test})$.
💡 Hints
Hint 1 — Label events
Let A = acidic, T = positive. $P(A)=0.005$, $P(T\mid A)=0.95$, $P(T\mid A')=0.01$.
Hint 2 — Total probability
$P(T)=P(T\mid A)P(A)+P(T\mid A')P(A')$
Hint 3 — Bayes' theorem
$P(A\mid T)=\dfrac{P(T\mid A)\,P(A)}{P(T)}$
✔ Answer
$P(T)=0.95\times0.005+0.01\times0.995=0.00475+0.00995=0.01470$
$P(A\mid T)=\dfrac{0.00475}{0.01470}\approx 0.323$ (about 32.3%)
Despite a positive result, only ~32% chance truly acidic — because acidic solutions are rare.
